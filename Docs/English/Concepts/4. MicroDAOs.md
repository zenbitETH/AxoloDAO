# MicroDAOs

A microDAO is the minimum viable version of a decentralized autonomous organization designed to give any small organization three foundational capabilities onchain: a verifiable identity, a shared treasury, and a permanent public record of its development.

Unlike the large DAOs described in [DAOs](./3.%20DAOs.md) that govern major protocols or coordinate thousands of token holders, a microDAO distills that institutional form into its smallest functional unit. It combines three Ethereum primitives: the Ethereum Name Service (ENS) for identity, Safe for shared treasury management, and the Ethereum Attestation Service (EAS) for onchain timestamping and verification. This combination allows startups, research collectives, and small businesses to acquire corporate identity, shared banking, and a public record of their development on a global network, enabling them to transition from informality at a fraction of the cost and time of traditional incorporation.<sup>[1](https://paragraph.com/@zenbit-2/how-to-onboard-startups-into-ethereum)</sup>

## 1. How a microDAO is formed

### a. Organization Identity with ENS

The Ethereum Name Service (ENS) is a decentralized naming system built on Ethereum that resolves human-readable names, such as `mycompany.eth`, to machine-readable Ethereum addresses and associated metadata.<sup>[2](https://docs.ens.domains/)</sup> In a microDAO context, a primary `.eth` domain functions as the organization's globally resolvable onchain identity: analogous to a registered legal name, but anchored to a public blockchain without a central authority capable of revoking, transferring, or censoring it.

ENS supports the creation of subdomains that extend the primary domain to individual members and organizational roles. In the latest standard, the primary `.eth` name resolves directly to the organization's Safe address, giving the treasury a human-readable identity without a separate subdomain. A microDAO might additionally register `member1.mycompany.eth` to identify a participant, or `node.mycompany.eth` to distinguish a functional unit. These subdomains create a named onchain organizational structure that resolves on every ENS-compatible application across the Ethereum ecosystem, without requiring a separate directory service, access control platform, or HR database. Because ENS names resolve on a permissionless network, they remain accessible regardless of whether any single application or infrastructure provider remains operational.<sup>[2](https://docs.ens.domains/)</sup>

### b. Organization Banking with Safe

Safe (formerly Gnosis Safe) is an open-source, audited smart contract framework for managing shared digital assets through multi-signature authorization.<sup>[3](https://docs.safe.global/)</sup> A Safe wallet requires a configurable threshold of signatures, for example, 2 of 3 or 3 of 5, to execute any transaction, meaning no single participant can unilaterally move funds. This reproduces the functional logic of a joint bank account or a board approval process, but without a bank, without a jurisdiction, and without the exclusion that comes with traditional financial gatekeeping.

Safe wallets are deployed as smart contracts that maintain the same address across all EVM-compatible networks (Ethereum mainnet, Optimism, Base, Arbitrum, and others), enabling a microDAO to hold and manage assets across multiple chains from a single shared interface. Assigning the organization's primary ENS name directly to the Safe address, for example, `mycompany.eth` pointing to a Safe on Optimism, gives the treasury a human-readable, publicly verifiable label that any participant can resolve onchain. This design makes the organization's financial infrastructure immediately legible: the name `mycompany.eth` and the shared wallet are one and the same from the first transaction.<sup>[1](https://paragraph.com/@zenbit-2/how-to-onboard-startups-into-ethereum)</sup>

### c. Organization Timestamping with EAS

The Ethereum Attestation Service (EAS) is an open protocol for creating, verifying, and querying cryptographically signed statements, called attestations, about any subject on or off Ethereum.<sup>[4](https://attest.org/)</sup> In a microDAO, EAS attestations function as the organization's permanent public record: signed, queryable, timestamped entries that document organizational events such as formation, milestone completion, governance decisions, funding rounds, or member role changes.

Unlike corporate records stored in proprietary databases or notarial archives, EAS attestations are globally accessible, cryptographically attributed to the issuing address, and portable across any tool or jurisdiction capable of querying the Ethereum network or its L2s. An organization can self-issue attestations documenting its own activity, or receive attestations from external validators, academic institutions, regulatory bodies, partner organizations, each adding a publicly verifiable layer of credibility to its onchain record. The cumulative result is an organizational history that is resistant to falsification, censorship, and jurisdictional loss.<sup>[4](https://attest.org/)</sup>

## 2. MicroDAOs for startups and small business

Approximately 391 million companies worldwide are startups or small and medium-sized enterprises (SMEs), representing roughly 99% of the global business count.<sup>[1](https://paragraph.com/@zenbit-2/how-to-onboard-startups-into-ethereum)</sup> These organizations are distributed unevenly across regions and generate the majority of employment in every region they operate in.

| Region | SME Share | Informality Rate |
|---|---|---|
| 🌏 Asia | 51% | 22% |
| 🌍 Africa | 23% | 38% |
| 🌍 Europe | ~10% | 15% |
| 🌎 North America | ~10% | 16% |
| 🌎 South America | 5% | 35% |
| 🌏 Oceania | 1% | 11% |

A structural problem confronting a significant share of these organizations is informality: the absence of legal entities, auditable accounts, cap tables, or formal agreements.<sup>[1](https://paragraph.com/@zenbit-2/how-to-onboard-startups-into-ethereum)</sup> In Mexico and Latin America, this challenge is particularly acute. Formal business incorporation involves notarial fees, regulatory registration, corporate account requirements, and compliance costs that together can represent several months of revenue for a new organization, a structural barrier that effectively excludes a large segment of the productive population from formal economic participation.

MicroDAOs address this barrier directly. By replacing legal entity registration with an ENS domain, traditional banking with a Safe multisig wallet, and notarial records with EAS attestations, a microDAO allows any organization to establish a verifiable onchain presence at a fraction of the cost. Zenbit's operational research with incubated startups and small organizations has identified a 95–99% reduction in both time and financial cost relative to traditional incorporation pathways in Mexico and Latin America.<sup>[1](https://paragraph.com/@zenbit-2/how-to-onboard-startups-into-ethereum)</sup> This positions microDAOs not as a replacement for legal structures where they are required, but as a practical formalization layer for organizations currently operating informally.

Within Geoffrey Moore's technology adoption framework, startups and SMEs occupy the early majority category, pragmatists who adopt technology to solve concrete operational problems rather than for novelty. The relevant problems microDAOs address are cost reduction, operational transparency, and global coordination access: three structural constraints that disproportionately affect organizations in emerging economies.<sup>[1](https://paragraph.com/@zenbit-2/how-to-onboard-startups-into-ethereum)</sup>

Based on Zenbit's experience incubating organizations through the microDAO model, a six-phase progression has proven effective as a general adoption framework for startups and small businesses entering the Ethereum ecosystem:

1. **MicroDAO creation**: Register a Safe multisig wallet and an ENS domain, and link the two by assigning the ENS name to the Safe address.
2. **Public presentation**: Publish an organizational introduction through a decentralized content platform such as Mirror, establishing a first verifiable public record.
3. **Operational goal definition**: Document the organization's near-term objectives and governance model, creating a reference point for future attestations and treasury decisions.
4. **Treasury management design**: Define the treasury logic, who signs, what thresholds are required, and how funds are allocated across operational goals.
5. **Onchain implementation**: Record organizational activities, milestones, agreements, partnerships, as EAS attestations, building an auditable onchain history.
6. **Onchain funding integration**: Connect the organization's treasury to available onchain funding mechanisms, including grants, retroactive funding programs, or DeFi-native savings and credit tools.

This progression is deliberately incremental. Organizations do not need to complete all six phases to derive value from the model; each phase adds a composable layer of onchain infrastructure that remains independently useful.<sup>[1](https://paragraph.com/@zenbit-2/how-to-onboard-startups-into-ethereum)</sup>

In its manual form, microDAO creation (phase one) requires approximately 30 to 45 minutes and involves four applications: smol.xyz to deploy a multichain Safe, the ENS dapp to register a primary domain and subdomains, the Safe dapp to assign the ENS name to the Safe address (requiring approval from each multisig member), and an EAS-compatible application to issue the first onchain attestation.

### Startupchain

Startupchain is a product developed by Zenbit that compresses the full microDAO creation process from 30–45 minutes to approximately two minutes. The underlying microDAO smart contracts were first prototyped in June 2025<sup>[6](https://github.com/zenbitETH/microDAO)</sup>; Startupchain itself was prototyped in August 2025.<sup>[5](https://github.com/zenbitETH/startupchain)</sup>

Rather than navigating four separate applications, Startupchain bundles ENS domain registration, multichain Safe creation, EAS attestation setup, and optional DeFi integrations, including yield-generating savings and collateralized lending, into a single unified onboarding flow. Privy handles wallet connection for participating organizations, enabling founders to link their existing Ethereum accounts to the onboarding flow without additional setup.<sup>[5](https://github.com/zenbitETH/startupchain)</sup>

```
┌─────────────┐    ┌─────────────┐    ┌─────────────┐    ┌─────────────┐

│  ENS Name   │    │  Founders   │    │  One-Time   │    │  Dashboard  │
└─────────────┘    └─────────────┘    └─────────────┘    └─────────────┘
                                             │
                          ┌──────────────────┴──────────────────┐
                          │  StartupChain handles automatically: │
                          │  • ENS commit → wait 60s → register │
                          │  • Deploy Safe multisig             │
                          │  • Record company on-chain          │
                          └─────────────────────────────────────┘
```

At ETHGlobal New York, Startupchain's prototype demonstrated compression of the ENS registration workflow from five steps to two, and received third place in the Best Use of ENS track.<sup>[7](https://ethglobal.com/showcase/startupchain-0fyf5)</sup> The underlying microDAO contracts are available as open-source infrastructure for independent deployment and extension.<sup>[6](https://github.com/zenbitETH/microDAO)</sup>

## 3. MicroDAO Use Cases

### Zenbit

Zenbit is a digital public infrastructure studio focused on Ethereum-based public goods and biodiversity conservation technology. It developed the microDAO concept through its own organizational formation and subsequently formalized it into an incubation program for organizations seeking to enter the Ethereum ecosystem.

Zenbit's incubation program guides participating organizations through the six-phase microDAO adoption framework described above. Each phase is documented through a progressive attestation badge system, eight badges issued onchain that collectively trace an organization's journey from Web3 introduction through technical development and onchain funding integration. Badges covering introduction to Web3, DAO formation, DAO presentation, operational goal definition, community building, decentralized funding, academic research, and technical development are issued as EAS attestations and are publicly queryable, providing both the incubated organization and external observers with a verifiable record of the organization's onchain maturity.<sup>[8](https://paragraph.com/@zenbit-2/zenbit-axolotarium-collaboration)</sup>

### Axolotarium

Axolotarium is a conservation center dedicated to the care and study of Mexican axolotls (*Ambystoma mexicanum*) and the first organization to complete the microDAO model through Zenbit's incubation program, beginning in 2024. The Axolotarium pilot demonstrated that the microDAO pattern was viable for conservation-focused institutions, not only for technology startups.<sup>[8](https://paragraph.com/@zenbit-2/zenbit-axolotarium-collaboration)</sup>

Through the incubation program, Axolotarium registered `axolotarium.eth` as its primary onchain identity, established a multichain Safe as its treasury, and published an organizational introduction on Mirror. It received three Zenbit badges documenting its progress: introduction to Web3, DAO formation, and DAO presentation. The pilot also identified a structural limitation in early microDAO design: using a Safe as the organization's primary identity, rather than as a labeled treasury component, created ambiguity between governance identity and financial infrastructure. Later iterations resolved this limitation by placing ENS at the center of organizational identity.<sup>[1](https://paragraph.com/@zenbit-2/how-to-onboard-startups-into-ethereum)</sup>

### AxoloDAO

AxoloDAO is a microDAO dedicated to conservation and scientific research for *Ambystoma* species in Mexico, and the primary organizational iteration in which the microDAO model was refined following the Axolotarium pilot. The principal architectural change was centering the organization's onchain identity on its ENS domain, `axolodao.eth`, rather than on its Safe, with the Safe clearly labeled as a treasury component. This structure makes the organization's architecture legible to any participant or external observer: the name `axolodao.eth` represents the organization; `treasury.axolodao.eth` represents its financial infrastructure; and member subdomains (`zenbit.axolodao.eth`, `xolotlcalli.axolodao.eth`, `ndali.axolodao.eth`) identify individual governance nodes.<sup>[9](https://paragraph.com/@zenbit-2/we-are-axolodao)</sup>

The onchain structure of AxoloDAO, verified from ENS registries on Ethereum mainnet and OP Mainnet, is organized as follows:

| ENS Domain | Resolved Address | Role |
|---|---|---|
| axolodao.eth | `0xcF1aeb4152F01e7643f772F4A08E0e791160B96B` | Primary administrative identity |
| treasury.axolodao.eth | `0xbDF701408aD237faA2d5d3177ae257aF35265dbb` | Safe multisig treasury (OP Mainnet) |
| zenbit.axolodao.eth | `0xeCB4C1245665e8A1F43826355aaB0Dd6bF336e05` | Infrastructure and system design node |
| xolotlcalli.axolodao.eth | `0xB5B5d7ba8c4474F66F7F86A348B1cE303a4BA02e` | Conservation and biomuseum node |
| ndali.axolodao.eth | — | Community articulation and coordination node |
| lups-plantae.axolodao.eth | — | Additional participant node |
| lego-lvsg.axolodao.eth | — | Additional participant node |

The AxoloDAO treasury (`treasury.axolodao.eth`) is a Safe multisig deployed on OP Mainnet, created by `zenbit.eth` on August 8, 2025. As of March 2026, the treasury has executed 94 transactions, with holdings concentrated on OP Mainnet and a minor position on Base. Distributions to member nodes are executed through batch transfers, with each node maintaining a subdomain-labeled address that is independently verifiable onchain.<sup>[10](https://optimistic.etherscan.io/address/0xbDF701408aD237faA2d5d3177ae257aF35265dbb)</sup>

AxoloDAO's conservation node, Xolotlcalli BioMuseum (`xolotlcalli.axolodao.eth`), has been the primary beneficiary of treasury disbursements since the DAO's formation. The address has recorded 40 onchain transactions since August 2025, receiving regular operational funding from the AxoloDAO treasury in support of the Biomuseum's physical conservation infrastructure, specimen care facilities, equipment, and field conservation programs for critically endangered *Ambystoma* species. Through combined contributions from Zenbit and the AxoloDAO treasury, over 60% of Biomuseum Xolotlcalli's operational infrastructure has been funded onchain, constituting a documented instance of Ethereum-based public goods funding enabling direct conservation outcomes.<sup>[9](https://paragraph.com/@zenbit-2/we-are-axolodao)</sup><sup>, [11](https://optimistic.etherscan.io/address/0xB5B5d7ba8c4474F66F7F86A348B1cE303a4BA02e)</sup>

AxoloDAO also maintains a set of offchain affiliations that complement its onchain structure. The Academic Body of Ecology and Wildlife Diversity (CAEyDF) contributes scientific research capacity; the Ministry of Environment and Natural Resources (SEMARNAT) provides regulatory alignment for protected species programs; the Secretary of Culture of the State of Querétaro (SECULT) connects the initiative to public cultural programming; and the São Paulo Institute of Technology and Leadership (INTELI) collaborates on system design and research methodology.
