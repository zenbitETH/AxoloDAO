# AxoloDAO Micro-DAO Experiment: Axolobabies
## 1. Context and Objective

As part of an ex-situ conservation initiative, our facility has received 10, 3-week old axolotl larvae (*Ambystoma mexicanum.*). The objective of this dynamic is to operate within a Web3 Testnet environment, managing digital assets inside a test-net (**ETH** and **USDC**) to sustain the biological and physicochemical requirements of these specimens. The survivance of the larvae will entirely depend on the actions and decisions made by the team in charge of each of the 4 different confinements.

To maintain the aquatic systems, your team must acquire funding through scientific data generation and strategic planning, subsequently allocating those resources to cover operational costs.

---

## 2. Revenue Generation: Scientific Methodologies

To earn capital in the Testnet, your team must successfully execute the following scientific tasks, meticulously logging the data:

### Task A: Morphometric Assessment Design
You must design and implement a non-invasive, low-stress protocol to accurately measure and weigh the juvenile specimens you´re asigned with. The system must capture the following specific variables:
*   **Total Length (LT):** Measured in millimeters (mm).
*   **Snout-Vent Length (LHC):** Measured in millimeters (mm).
*   **Body Mass:** Measured in grams (g).
*   **Body Condition Index (ICC):** Calculated to assess the nutritional status of the specimens.

### Task B: Behavioral Ethograms
You must conduct standardized observational sessions (e.g., 10-minute intervals) to monitor the specimens' welfare. Your ethograms must systematically record specific behavioral states and events, including:
1.  *Reposo* (Resting)
2.  *Locomoción* (Locomotion/Displacement)
3.  *Ocultación* (Hiding)
4.  *Superficie* (Surface breathing)
5.  *Superficial buoyancy* (time spent floating in the surface of the water)

### Task C: Financial Sustainability Strategy
Design a long-term financial strategy to fund the "axolobabies." Propose innovative Web3 funding mechanisms within the Micro-DAO framework to ensure continuous resource generation for system upkeep.

---

## 3. Resource Allocation: System Maintenance

The Testnet ETH/USDC earned from your scientific tasks must be managed efficiently. You will use your capital to procure the following operational supplies:

### 3.1 Nutritional Inputs
Purchasing high-quality biomass strictly recorded in feeding logs. Approved dietary options available for purchase include:

*   *Daphnia magna*
*   *Tubifex* (live or freeze-dried)
*   *Artemia spp.*

### 3.2 Water Quality Diagnostics
Purchasing chemical reagents and testing kits to monitor critical physicochemical parameters. The aquatic environment must strictly adhere to the following target ranges:

| Parameter | Target Range | Critical Threshold (Alarm) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Temperature** | 15.0°C – 18.0°C | > 18.0°C |
| **Ammonia (NH3)** | 0 ppm | > 0.25 ppm |
| **Nitrites (NO2)**| 0 ppm | > 0.1 ppm |
| **Nitrates (NO3)**| < 20 ppm | > 20 ppm |
| **pH** | 7.0 – 7.5 | < 7.0 or > 7.5 |

### 3.3 System Operations
In order to found operational tasks, such as periodic water changes, purchasing buffer solutions, and filtration system maintenance (mechanical and biological). Students will have to gain enough capital to pay for each of the activities (Cost will be based on real operational costs). 

## 4. Evaluation Criteria
Your success in this Micro-DAO experiment will be measured by two primary indicators:
1.  **Biological Success:** The physical growth, maintenance of a healthy Body Condition Index (ICC), and absence of stress indicators (e.g., abnormal floating, excessive mucus) in the assigned axolotls.
2.  **Financial Viability:** The efficiency of your resource allocation and the robustness of the DAO's treasury management.
